# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CPA advisors at small-to-mid advisory firms. They use Folio daily for 6-8 hours while managing 20-100+ clients. Technically proficient but not developers. Time-pressured: they switch between clients quickly and often have the software open during client meetings. Two modes: (1) working mode — reviewing data before a meeting, (2) presentation mode — walking a client through their tax savings on the Snapshot tab.

## Product Purpose

Folio is a CPA client portal that turns tax strategy management from a spreadsheet exercise into a real-time advisory workflow. CPAs use it to track estimated quarterly payments, activate tax-saving strategies, and show clients what's been saved and what's still on the table. Success looks like: a CPA walking into a client meeting with instant confidence in the numbers, and walking out with the client fully understanding their tax picture.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, premium. The voice is that of a confident, senior partner — never showy, never hand-holdy. It earns trust through accuracy and clarity, not through motion or personality. It's the design equivalent of a well-pressed suit.

## Anti-references

- **Old-school accounting software (QuickBooks, Drake Tax)** — functional but visually dated, no design ambition, looks like it was built in 2003. Folio should feel like the industry has moved on.
- **Consumer tax software (TurboTax)** — hand-holdy, bright, designed for non-experts. Folio users are experts; the UX should assume that.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — clean but identity-free. Folio has a distinct brand (forest green, gold, serif numerals); it should look like it belongs to a specific, considered firm.

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over personality.** Every pixel should communicate accuracy. Decorative choices that don't carry information should be cut.
2. **Data density earns trust.** CPAs trust software that shows them everything, not software that hides complexity behind progressive disclosure. Dense layouts are a feature, not a problem.
3. **The Snapshot tab is sacred.** It's what CPAs show clients. Every design decision on that screen must survive the question: "would I be embarrassed if this was on screen during a client meeting?"
4. **Speed over ceremony.** The software is used under time pressure. Navigation should be immediate. No loading spinners where a skeleton suffices. No modals where inline works.
5. **Premium is earned by restraint.** Gold and serif type are used for what matters most — totals, client names, key savings numbers. Not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected (already implemented via prefers-reduced-motion media query and useCountUp JS check). Primary input: keyboard + mouse on desktop. No mobile requirement (CPA workstations). Focus rings required for all interactive elements.
